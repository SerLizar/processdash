PROBE Methods A, B, and C all draw a line describing some trend in your historical data. The following parameters describe that line:

Beta0: describes how far away from zero the line is when it crosses the vertical axis.

Beta1: describes the slope, or steepness, of the line.

r2: describes how well your historical data points fall along the line. When r2 is 0.9 or greater, the line describes your data points very well. When r2 is less than 0.5, your data points are too scattered for the line to be meaningful.

p: Sometimes data points fall along a line by accident. The number p gives the probability that this has occurred. So if p were 60%, it would mean that there was a 60% chance that your data points just fell along the given line randomly, and only a 40% chance that the line describes some real relationship in your data.
